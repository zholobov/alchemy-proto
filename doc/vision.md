# Alchemy Game — Vision

## Core Fantasy

You are an alchemist. Your workbench holds a stone mortar, a shelf of ingredients, and your curiosity. You drop, pour, and dispense substances into the mortar and watch as they interact — not through scripted animations, but through a live simulation of their physical and chemical properties.

Sulfur ignites. Acid dissolves metal. Pressure builds. Things explode.

No recipes. No crafting menus. The simulation is the game.

## What Makes This Different

Most alchemy/crafting games are combinatorial puzzles: put A + B in a slot, get C. The player memorizes recipes.

This game simulates the *why*. Sulfur burns because it's flammable and its temperature exceeded its flash point. Acid dissolves iron because acid is reactive and iron is a base metal. An explosion happens because gas was produced faster than it could escape, pressure built up, and the container failed.

The player learns the system, not a recipe book. Mastery comes from understanding properties and predicting interactions.

## Prototype Goals

1. **Performance validation** — Can the simulation run at acceptable framerates with enough particles to look good?
2. **Reaction system proof** — Do emergent property-based interactions produce interesting, varied, and visually distinct results?
3. **Interaction loop** — Does picking up substances, dropping them in, and watching results feel satisfying?

## Long-Term Vision

- Multiple receptacle types with different properties (glass, iron, crystal)
- Progression: discover new substances through experimentation
- Goals/challenges: "produce this substance" or "reach this temperature"
- Painterly visual style with magical atmosphere
- Sound design that makes reactions feel visceral
- Possibly: multiplayer lab where players collaborate or compete
